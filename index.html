About the Journal

The Journal of Materials Intelligence and Computing (JMIC) is an interdisciplinary, peer-reviewed journal dedicated to advancing research at the convergence of materials science, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and computational modeling.

Aims

JMIC aims to serve as a premier interdisciplinary forum for research integrating materials science with AI, machine learning, and computational modeling. The journal focuses on accelerating materials discovery, optimization, and understanding through data-driven and physics-based approaches.

The journal publishes transformative research addressing grand challenges in materials science, including novel material prediction, process optimization, structure–property relationships, and autonomous experimentation.

Scope

1. Materials Informatics & Data Science

  • Materials databases, ontologies, and data standards
  • High-throughput screening and virtual libraries
  • Data mining, feature engineering, and descriptor development
  • Transfer learning and multi-fidelity modeling

2. Machine Learning & AI for Materials

  • Supervised and unsupervised learning for materials prediction
  • Deep learning for molecules, crystals, and microstructures
  • Generative models for inverse materials design
  • Explainable AI (XAI)

3. Computational Modeling & Simulation

  • DFT, molecular dynamics, and Monte Carlo simulations
  • Phase-field modeling and finite element analysis
  • Multi-scale modeling
  • Uncertainty quantification

4. AI + Physics Integration

  • Physics-informed machine learning
  • Hybrid and surrogate modeling
  • Model reduction techniques

5. Autonomous Materials Discovery

  • Self-driving laboratories
  • Active learning and Bayesian optimization
  • Closed-loop experimentation

6. Applications

  • Energy materials, nanomaterials, and biomaterials
  • Functional and structural materials

7. Software & Reproducibility

  • Open-source tools and workflows
  • FAIR data principles
  • Benchmarking and validation
